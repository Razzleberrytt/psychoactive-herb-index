Kava Kava

Dissociative \/ Sedative

Body numbing, mind floating

Moderate

Mechanism of Action
Kavalactones such as kavain, methysticin, and yangonin modulate GABAA receptor activity and inhibit voltage-gated ion channels, producing anxiolytic, sedative, and muscle relaxant effects [1, 3, 1, 0].
Pharmacokinetics
Traditional aqueous extracts: onset 20–40 min; peak plasma kavalactone levels ~1–2 h; extensively metabolized hepatically [1, 0].
Therapeutic Uses
Ceremonial and social drink in Pacific cultures for anxiety relief, muscle relaxation, and mild euphoria [1, 0].
Side Effects
Drowsiness, headache, gastrointestinal discomfort; potential hepatotoxicity with chronic use of non-water extracts [1, 0, 1, 6].
Contraindications
Liver disease, concurrent alcohol or hepatotoxic medications, pregnancy.
Drug Interactions
Additive sedation with alcohol and CNS depressants; caution with anticoagulants [1, 0].
Toxicity
Human LD50 unknown; animal studies indicate moderate safety margin; heavy chronic use linked to rare hepatotoxicity [1, 6].
