Mechanism of Action
Steroidal saponins (shatavarins) modulate estrogen receptors and exhibit antioxidant, immunomodulatory, and neuroprotective properties [1, 4].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral administration: onset 30–60 min; bioactive saponins have prolonged half-life; cumulative effects with daily dosing [1, 4].
Therapeutic Uses
Galactagogue, reproductive health support, adaptogen for stress, gastric ulcer treatment, diuretic [1, 5].
Side Effects
GI upset, headache; potential hormonal effects at high doses [1, 5].
Contraindications
Pregnancy (high doses), hormone-sensitive cancers; caution with hypoglycemic agents [0, 3].
Drug Interactions
Lithium (efficacy reduction) and hypoglycemic drugs; possible additive effects [0, 3].
Toxicity
Low toxicity; very high doses may induce convulsions in animal models; generally well-tolerated [1, 9].
