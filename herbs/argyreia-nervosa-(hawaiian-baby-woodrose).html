Argyreia nervosa (Hawaiian Baby Woodrose)

Category
Ethnobotanical \/ Rare
Effects
Visionary, sedative, nausea-prone
Preparation
Cold water seed infusion
Intensity
High
Onset
30-90 min
Legal Status
Restricted in some countries
Region
?? Unknown \/ Global
Tags
⚠️ Restricted
Mechanism of Action
Seeds contain ergolines (ergine\/LSA and isoergine) acting as partial agonists at 5-HT₂A serotonin receptors and dopamine receptors, producing mild psychedelic effects [1, 2, 1, 7].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral ingestion: onset 20–180 min; peak ~2 h; duration 4–10 h [1, 2].
Therapeutic Uses
Traditional entheogen in Mesoamerican rituals and modern recreational use [1, 2].
Side Effects
Nausea, vomiting, vasoconstriction, dizziness, sedation [1, 2].
Contraindications
Cardiovascular disorders, psychiatric conditions, pregnancy.
Drug Interactions
CNS depressants and serotonergic agents may enhance effects.
Toxicity
No known human fatalities; rodent LD50 for ergine ~200–300 mg\/kg.
