Valerian Root

Category: Dissociative / Sedative

Effects: Deep sedation, dream fog

Preparation: Tea or tincture

Intensity: Mild

Onset: 15-45 min

Legal Status: Legal / Unregulated

Region: 🇪🇺 Europe

Tags: ☕ Brewable, 🌀 Dissociation, ✅ Safe

Mechanism of Action: None

Pharmacokinetics: Oral extracts: onset 30–60 min; constituents metabolized hepatically; half-life varies by preparation [4, 0].

Therapeutic Uses: Insomnia, nervous tension, mild anxiety; often combined with other sedatives for sleep disorders [4, 0].

Side Effects: Headache, stomach upset, vivid dreams, excitability; rare withdrawal symptoms upon abrupt discontinuation [4, 4].

Contraindications: Pregnancy, lactation; caution in those with hepatic impairment [4, 8].

Drug Interactions: Additive sedation with barbiturates, benzodiazepines, CNS depressants; may increase effects of amobarbital and phenobarbital [4, 7].

Toxicity: Low; occasional reports of hepatotoxicity, safe at therapeutic doses [4, 4].

Toxicity / LD50: None
