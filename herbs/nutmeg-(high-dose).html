Mechanism of Action
Myristicin and elemicin exhibit mild hallucinogenic and anticholinergic effects by inhibiting acetylcholine receptors and monoamine oxidase [0, 21].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral ingestion: onset 3–6 h; duration up to 12 h; metabolized hepatically, excreted renally [0, 21].
Therapeutic Uses
Traditional deliriant, dream enhancement, aphrodisiac; flavoring agent in culinary contexts [0, 21].
Side Effects
Nausea, dry mouth, anxiety, delirium, hallucinations at psychoactive doses [0, 21].
Contraindications
Psychiatric disorders, liver disease, pregnancy [0, 21].
Drug Interactions
Additive CNS effects with depressants and MAOIs [0, 21].
Toxicity
Moderate-to-high toxicity at psychoactive doses; overdose risk for anticholinergic delirium [0, 21].
Toxicity \/ LD50
Estimated LD50 (myristicin) ~5600 mg\/kg in rats; toxic threshold in humans ~5g+ of nutmeg powder.
