Anadenanthera peregrina (Yopo)

Category
Ethnobotanical \/ Rare
Effects
Powerful snuff, DMT-like visuals
Preparation
Prepared as snuff (contains bufotenine)
Intensity
High
Onset
1-3 min
Legal Status
Restricted in many regions
Region
???? Latin America
Tags
⚠️ Restricted
Mechanism of Action
Bufotenin in the seeds functions as a non-selective serotonin receptor agonist and serotonin releasing agent [1, 1].
Pharmacokinetics
Insufflated snuff: rapid onset; typical dose contains ~40 mg bufotenin from ~0.5 g seeds; rodent LD50 ~200–300 mg\/kg [1, 1].
Therapeutic Uses
Used ceremonially as cohoba\/yopo snuff for entheogenic experiences [1, 1].
Side Effects
Tachycardia, facial flushing, respiratory difficulty, leg cramps, nausea, vomiting [1, 1].
Contraindications
Cardiovascular disease, psychiatric disorders, pregnancy.
Drug Interactions
Serotonergic medications may potentiate effects.
Toxicity
High concentrations potentially embryotoxic and angiogenic; human toxicity data limited.
Toxicity \/ LD50
Bufotenin LD50 (mice, intraperitoneal): ~200–300 mg\/kg; relatively safe at ritual doses.
