Mechanism of Action
Coumarins (dimethylfraxetin) and flavonoids modulate serotonergic (5-HT1A) and GABAergic (benzodiazepine binding) neurotransmission, producing anxiolytic and sedative-like effects [0, 0, 0, 4].
Pharmacokinetics
Aqueous infusions or tinctures: onset ~30–60 min; active coumarins undergo hepatic metabolism; excreted renally [0, 2].
Therapeutic Uses
Traditional antispasmodic, antidiarrheal, anxiolytic, sedative, and additive in tobacco mixtures for psychoactive effects [0, 0, 0, 5].
Side Effects
Drowsiness, nausea, headache in high doses [0, 1].
Contraindications
Pregnancy, lactation, respiratory conditions (smoking) [0, 0].
