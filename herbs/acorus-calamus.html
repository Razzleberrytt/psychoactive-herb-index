Category
Dissociative \/ Sedative
Effects
Mild hallucinations, odd clarity

Mild

15-45 min

Mechanism of Action
Exhibits neuroprotective, antioxidant, antidepressant, and anticonvulsant effects, likely via modulation of GABAergic and monoaminergic systems [0, 10, 0, 15].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral administration: onset 30–60 min; peak plasma levels within 1–2 h; metabolized hepatically and excreted renally [0, 15].
Therapeutic Uses
Traditional use as digestive tonic, neuroprotectant, and mood regulator; modern research explores antidepressant and anticonvulsant applications [0, 10].
Side Effects
Potential emmenagogic and genotoxic activity; avoid use due to carcinogenic β-asarone content [0, 0, 0, 5].
Contraindications
Pregnancy and lactation; use prohibited in some regions [0, 0].
Drug Interactions
None well documented; may reduce effectiveness of antacids [0, 25].
Toxicity
High doses linked to neurotoxicity; banned in some countries due to carcinogenic concerns [0, 0].
