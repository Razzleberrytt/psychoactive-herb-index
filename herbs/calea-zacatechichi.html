Mechanism of Action
Effects attributed to flavones and germacrolides modulating cholinergic and possibly serotonergic pathways, enhancing dream-related neurochemistry [0, 1, 0, 11].
Pharmacokinetics
Consumed as bitter tea or smoked: onset 20–45 min; duration ~2–3 h; likely metabolized hepatically [0, 1, 0, 21].
Therapeutic Uses
Traditional Mexican use for dream enhancement, divination, and improvement of dream recall [0, 6].
Side Effects
Bitter taste, nausea, vomiting; mild sedation [0, 6].
Contraindications
Avoid during pregnancy and combine cautiously with sedatives [0, 16].
Drug Interactions
Potentiates CNS depressants [0, 21].
Toxicity
Low reported toxicity; high doses may cause dizziness or vomiting [0, 16].
