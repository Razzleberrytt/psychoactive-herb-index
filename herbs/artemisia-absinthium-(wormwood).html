Mechanism of Action
Contains (−)-α- and (+)-β-thujone, potent GABAₐ receptor antagonists; also exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and MAO-inhibitory activities [1, 3, 0, 7].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral use: onset 20–60 min; peak plasma levels ~1–2 h; systemic distribution of active constituents [0, 22].
Therapeutic Uses
Digestive tonic, Crohn's disease aid, antiparasitic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory agent [0, 7].
Side Effects
High-dose thujone can cause convulsions, insomnia, and hallucinations; GI upset; allergic reactions [1, 3, 0, 7].
Contraindications
Pregnancy, epilepsy, seizure disorders, liver disease; avoid in children [0, 7].
Drug Interactions
Increases INR with warfarin; additive CNS depression with sedatives and MAOIs [1, 3].
Toxicity
Rodent LD50 for thujone ~45 mg\/kg; chronic overuse linked to neurotoxicity and convulsions [1, 3].
