Mechanism of Action
Contains (−)-α- and (+)-β-thujone, which act as GABAA receptor antagonists, causing mild convulsant and excitatory effects; also exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties [0, 3, 0, 22].
Pharmacokinetics
Oral use: onset 20–60 min; peak plasma thujone ~1–2 h; metabolized by CYP450 enzymes [0, 22].
Therapeutic Uses
Digestive tonic, antiparasitic, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory; ingredient in traditional absinthe liquor [0, 7].
Side Effects
Nausea, vomiting, convulsions, hallucinations at high doses; GI upset; allergic reactions [0, 3, 0, 22].
Contraindications
Pregnancy, epilepsy, seizure disorders, liver disease.
Drug Interactions
Additive neurotoxicity with other GABA antagonists; caution with CNS stimulants [0, 17].
Toxicity
Rodent oral LD50 for thujone ~45 mg\/kg; chronic high intake associated with neurotoxicity [0, 3].
