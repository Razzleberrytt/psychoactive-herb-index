# How to Prepare Kava: Ritual, Relaxation, and Respect

Kava is more than just a calming drink — it's a cultural cornerstone in the Pacific Islands, where it's used for ceremonies, social bonding, and stress relief. If you're new to kava, here's how to prepare it with both care and intention.

## 🌱 What Is Kava?

Kava (Piper methysticum) is a root used to produce a drink with mild sedative and anti-anxiety effects. The active compounds, called kavalactones, interact with GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation without impairing mental clarity.

## 🧴 Ingredients & Tools

- Ground kava root (noble variety)
- Cold or lukewarm water (not hot!)
- A strainer bag or muslin cloth
- A large bowl or container

## 🌀 Step-by-Step Preparation

1. **Add Kava to the Bag**: Use 1–2 tablespoons per person.
2. **Add Water**: About 1 cup of water per serving.
3. **Massage and Knead**: Squeeze the bag in the water for 5–10 minutes. The liquid should turn muddy brown.
4. **Serve & Sip**: Traditionally, kava is consumed in a coconut shell. Take small sips and let the effects gently build.

## ✨ Respect the Plant

Kava is non-addictive and relatively safe, but it deserves respect. Avoid alcohol and don’t drive after drinking. Set a calm setting and drink slowly.

Let this ancient plant teach you the art of relaxation — island style.
