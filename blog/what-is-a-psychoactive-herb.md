# What Is a Psychoactive Herb (and How Are They Different from Drugs?)

When people hear the word “psychoactive,” they often think of something intense — LSD, mushrooms, or strong synthetic substances. But the truth is: many of us consume psychoactive herbs every single day. Coffee, for instance, is one of the most widely used psychoactive plants in the world.

## 🌿 Defining “Psychoactive”
A psychoactive substance is any compound that alters your mind — mood, perception, cognition, or behavior — by acting on the central nervous system. When that substance comes from a natural plant, root, flower, or fungus, we call it a psychoactive herb.

These include:
- **Stimulants** like kratom or guarana
- **Relaxants** like kava or blue lotus
- **Visionary agents** like salvia or ayahuasca

Some have subtle effects. Others? Not so much.

## 🌍 Natural vs. Synthetic
Many people think “natural” means safe and “synthetic” means dangerous. But it’s not that simple. Both natural and synthetic substances can be incredibly healing — or incredibly harmful — depending on dosage, intent, and context.

Natural doesn’t mean weak, and lab-made doesn’t mean evil. At Hippie Scientist, we believe the best approach is informed curiosity.

## 🧪 Herb ≠ Drug?
The difference between an herb and a drug is largely cultural and regulatory. Many herbs — like kratom or kanna — are used for therapeutic, ceremonial, or spiritual reasons in traditional cultures, but are misunderstood or criminalized elsewhere.

Our mission is to educate, de-stigmatize, and explore these plants in a grounded and science-literate way.
